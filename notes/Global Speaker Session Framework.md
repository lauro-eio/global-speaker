# Global Speaker Session Framework

---

## Phase 1: Context & Importance (15% of Session Time)

### Step 1: Introduction & Objective Alignment

**Activity:** The coach introduces the chapter topic, establishes the explicit "Work-Ready" objective, and details exactly why mastering this specific skill is critical to professional success.

**Execution:** Frame the commercial relevance and real-world high stakes of the session's macro-scenario (e.g., cross-border panel, investor pitching, or high-level networking). Focus entirely on why the upcoming objective matters to global leadership, without diagnosing specific flaws yet.

---

## Phase 2: Baseline Calibration (15% of Session Time)

### Step 2: The Initial Baseline Run

**Activity:** Every participant takes a turn executing the unassisted Input Baseline format once.

**Execution:** This establishes the behavioral starting point for the group. It allows both the coach and the participants to feel the immediate friction and limitations of the standard fallback or "credentials-first" approach before any transformation logic is introduced.

---

## Phase 3: Technical Demonstration (10% of Session Time)

### Step 3: The Coach Demonstration & Flaw Exposure

**Activity:** The coach delivers an expert live demonstration of the Target Output by applying the explicit Transformation Rules.

**Execution:** Now possessing the context of the students' baseline runs, the coach contrasts the common structural flaws observed during Step 2 against the optimized target standard. If the coach cannot generate a scenario on the spot, they must read an included, fully produced industry-specific example out loud to model the exact structural layout, timing, and linguistic triggers required to pass.

---

## Phase 4: Deliberate Practice & Peer Feedback Loops (60% of Session Time)

### Step 4: The Rotational Transformation Drill

**Activity:** The class rotates sequentially through the room, where every student must actively deliver their transformed speech.

**Execution Cycle:**

1. **The Active Listening Prompt:** Before a student begins, the coach prompts the rest of the class to listen intently, explicitly warning them that someone will be randomly selected to provide targeted feedback immediately after the delivery.
2. **The Speech Delivery:** The student delivers their full speech, applying the transformation framework to shift their communication style.
3. **The Peer Feedback Loop (60s Limit):** The coach randomly calls on one student to provide exactly one piece of articulate feedback. This peer feedback must focus strictly and exclusively on the specific objective of the session; generic or delivery-medium comments are banned.
4. **The Coach Calibration (60s Limit):** The coach delivers a rapid, sharp evaluation to complement the peer feedback, strictly checking against the specific Success Metrics (Technical Accuracy, Linguistic Fluency, and Executive Presence).
5. **The Quality Gate & Loop Repeat:** If the student fails the structural layout, they re-enter the queue. The cycle repeats continuously across the classroom roster until every participant's output meets the expected target standard.

---

## Phase 5: The Complexity Shift (Conditional Trigger)

### The Dynamic Pressure Layer

**Activity:** Stress-testing structural and behavioral durability under cognitive load.

**Execution:** This layer remains dormant and is only activated if and when 100% of the session participants have successfully achieved the satisfactory target goal under baseline conditions. Once triggered, the coach applies either a Time Crunch constraint or an Adversarial variable (e.g., live interruptions or objections) to the rotational loop.

---

## Phase 6: Validation & Reflection (Remaining Session Margin)

### Step 5: The Gains Check-In

**Activity:** A structured table-rotation where participants reflect on their transformation.

**Execution:** Go around the room and ask each participant how they feel to actively validate their individual skill gains. To maintain professional momentum, each participant is restricted to a strict time limit (e.g., 30–60 seconds) to express their thoughts and emotional shifts from baseline to target output.

---

## Phase 7: Executive Closing (Final 2-3 Minutes)

### Step 6: Congratulate & Dismiss

**Activity:** Final session wrap-up and energy surge.

**Execution:** Formally close the session. Thank every participant for their absolute focus and vulnerability during the drills, congratulate them on hitting the performance bar, and cheer the group on to build collective confidence for the upcoming work week.
